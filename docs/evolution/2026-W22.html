RESEARCH · FITS

Multi-Dimensional News Sentiment: Dispersion + Uncertainty Beat Polarity

Two 2026 papers converge on the same counter-intuitive result: net positive/negative tone is a weak predictor, but cross-article DISAGREEMENT (sentiment dispersion) and UNCERTAINTY language are the dominant alpha signals. GDELT + FinBERT + XGBoost on macro news achieves Sharpe ratios of 5.87 (EUR/USD), 4.65 (USD/JPY), and 4.65 (10-yr Treasuries) out-of-sample 2017–Apr 2025, with sentiment dispersion as the top SHAP feature (arxiv 2505.16136). For WTI crude oil, GPT-4o intensity mean and FinBERT polarity standard deviation — not net polarity — yield IC 0.2283 and AUC 0.6515 vs 0.5694 baseline (arxiv 2603.11408). Both papers flag hedging language and ambiguity as economically meaningful dimensions that simple FinBERT scores discard.

Implication. fits existing: news-reactive — replace or augment the morning-brief polarity score with dispersion (variance of scores across articles) and uncertainty-word density; sharp cross-source disagreement is itself the signal, not the direction of the consensus

RESEARCH · FITS

Hyperbolic Momentum Alpha Decay Accelerated by Factor ETF Crowding

A late-2025 paper derives a hyperbolic decay model alpha(t) = K / (1 + λt) that fits US equity momentum significantly better than linear (R²=0.65 vs 0.51) or exponential (R²=0.61) alternatives. The alarming finding: factor ETF AUM growth correlates ρ=−0.63 with crowding acceleration post-2015, and out-of-sample the model over-predicts remaining momentum alpha (model forecast 0.30, realised only 0.15). Mechanical factors (momentum, short-term reversal) crowd far faster than judgment-based ones (value, quality); this asymmetry is robust across sub-periods (arxiv 2512.11913).

Implication. fits existing: momentum-trader — momentum's effective holding period has compressed post-2015 due to ETF crowding; backtest whether tighter exits (1–2 days vs current 3–5) or a rolling factor-ETF-flow crowding proxy as a position-size gate recovers the Sharpe

RESEARCH · SPAWN CANDIDATE

Volume-Price Interactions and Multi-Period Mean Reversion Survive Strict Double-Selection LASSO

Testing 168 short-term Alpha191 signals against 151 fundamental control factors via double-selection LASSO on S&P 500 constituents (2002–2022, monthly rebalance), only 17 survive t > 2.0. Dominant survivors are volume-price interactions: Multi-Period Mean Reversion Ratio (79 bps/month, t = 3.68), 20-day cumulative On-Balance Volume (51 bps/month, t = 3.68), and overnight gap return (41 bps/month, t = 2.46). Counter-intuitively, the signals strengthen monotonically from 1-day to 21-day horizons — ruling out microstructure-noise explanations and suggesting persistent behavioural or liquidity premia (arxiv 2601.06499).

Implication. spawn-candidate: mean-reverter-v2 — add 20-day On-Balance Volume and overnight gap return as entry-confirmation filters layered on top of the existing RSI<30 + SMA20-distance signals; these two survive the strictest empirical controls the literature has applied to short-term technical factors
