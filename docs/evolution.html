RESEARCH · FITS

Volatility-Conditional Momentum: VIX Spikes Are the Primary Culprit

Haim Mozes ('Volatility Spikes and Momentum', Journal of Beta Investment Strategies, Spring 2026) documents that VIX spikes (defined as VIX ≥ 1.5× its 3-month MA) occurred 22 times in 2014-2024 vs only 7 times in 2004-2013. Momentum returns averaged -0.73%/month in spike months and the immediately following month, vs +0.54%/month otherwise; over the full 2014-2024 period, momentum averaged only 2.23% annualised — explaining the decade-long underperformance. The mechanism is that spike-driven reversals catch momentum portfolios positioned for continuation.

Implication. fits existing: momentum-trader — add a VIX-state gate (reduce gross exposure or pause new entries when VIX > 1.5× its 60-day MA; resume only after one calm month confirms the spike has passed)

RESEARCH · SPAWN CANDIDATE

Factor Crowding: Anomaly-Driven Demand Is Now Self-Referential

Posselt & Kjær ('Anomaly-Driven Demand', March 2026), covered by Larry Swedroe for Alpha Architect (May 22, 2026), demonstrate that factor rebalancing by the collective strategy cohort is large enough to mechanically move prices, particularly at month-start and in stocks sitting at the intersection of multiple anomaly portfolios simultaneously. A measurable fraction of observed factor alpha is generated by the coordinated buying of other factor investors, not underlying economic fundamentals — making it fragile to cohort shrinkage. Widely-followed screens (price momentum, low-vol, value overlap) are most affected.

Implication. spawn-candidate: a calendar-aware momentum variant that rebalances mid-month (e.g. 10th trading day) rather than end-of-month to avoid buying into the crowded demand pulse and selling into the unwind

RESEARCH · FITS

Dynamic Factor Timing via Factor Momentum: Validated on ETFs

Tai, Leung & Jimenez (SSRN 6224058, February 12, 2026) show that rotating between factor ETFs (value/momentum/quality/low-vol) using a simple factor-momentum signal — the same factor that outperformed recently continues to outperform — achieves a Sharpe of 0.66 vs 0.59 for equal-weight, 13.0% vs 11.3% annualised, with a 78.6% calendar-year hit rate over 1998-2025. Turnover is ~3× annually. Crucially, the authors validate the strategy on actual ETF prices and find only modest degradation from tracking error and expense ratios, confirming retail implementability.

Implication. fits existing: sector-rotator — upgrade the scoring function from raw sector ETF relative-strength to a factor-momentum-weighted composite, i.e. rank sector ETFs by the momentum of their recent momentum score rather than the score itself

RESEARCH · FITS

Residual vs Systematic Return Decomposition in Industry Momentum

Gao, Li, Yuan & Zhou (SSRN 6371558, March 8, 2026) decompose short-term returns into idiosyncratic and systematic components and find that while individual stocks exhibit 1-month reversal, industry, characteristic, and factor portfolios exhibit short-term momentum. A 'residual-to-systematic reversal' component is embedded within raw industry momentum, partially cancelling the signal and causing standard spanning regressions to misattribute its source. Hedging out this residual component substantially strengthens industry momentum profits and demonstrates that industry momentum is economically distinct from — and additive to — factor momentum.

Implication. fits existing: sector-rotator — when scoring sectors, subtract the cross-sectional stock-level reversal component (i.e. don't let a sector score be dragged down by its own internal short-term reversals); practically, score sectors using a cap-weighted-smoothed return rather than the simple average return across member stocks

RESEARCH · FITS

ESG Ratings as State-Dependent Drawdown Insurance

Yi, Hu, Sun & Chen (arXiv 2605.04479, May 6, 2026; NTU / Cornell / JHU / UT Austin) use Double Machine Learning to deconfound selection bias and show that high-ESG portfolios materially reduce discrete crash incidence during systemic drawdowns. Protection is concentrated at adverse quantiles only — not a full-distribution shift — and comes with a measurable performance drag in stable regimes. This is the first paper to identify ESG protection as *priced* insurance (the drag is the premium) rather than free alpha, which changes the framing from 'ESG factor' to 'drawdown hedge'.

Implication. fits existing: macro-aligned — use an ESG quality tier (e.g. MSCI ESG rating ≥ AA) as an additional stock-universe filter that activates only in stressed regimes (yield-curve inversion or DXY risk-off), trading some upside for a lower crash-incidence profile
