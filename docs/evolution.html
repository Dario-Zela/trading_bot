RESEARCH · FITS

VIX-Spike Frequency Has Structurally Impaired Unconditional Momentum

Haim Mozes, 'Volatility Spikes and Momentum,' Journal of Beta Investment Strategies Vol 17:1 (Spring 2026), analysed 1994–2024 in three equal decades. VIX spikes tripled in the most recent decade (22 events vs 7 in 2004–2013) and now reverse almost twice as fast (28 trading days avg vs 54). Momentum loses the bulk of its annual premium during spike regimes; with spikes more frequent and faster-reversing, unconditional momentum faces a persistent structural headwind rather than a cyclical slump. The mechanism is involuntary deleveraging and crowded-trade unwinds, not factor decay per se.

Implication. fits existing: momentum-trader

RESEARCH · FITS

Per-Factor Momentum-Regime Z-Score Lifts Factor Allocation Sharpe by 12%

Tai, Leung & Jimenez (SSRN abstract 6224058, posted Feb 2026) apply a two-hyperparameter z-score to identify per-factor Bull/Bear regimes across Value, Size, Momentum, Quality, and Growth over 1998–2025. The regime-timed portfolio achieves Sharpe 0.66 vs 0.59 equal-weight and 13.0% annualised return vs 11.3%, with Size and Growth regime signals significant at p < 0.01. The key novelty is per-factor granularity: a single macro Bull/Bear flag misses the orthogonal regime structure of each factor.

Implication. fits existing: macro-aligned

RESEARCH · SPAWN CANDIDATE

LLM Extraction of Semantic Shifts in Corporate Filings Yields >2× Risk-Adjusted Alpha

Choi et al. (arXiv 2510.03195, revised March 2026) build an 'LLM as extractor, embedding as ruler' pipeline on SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K). The model identifies 'moving targets'—firms that quietly shift emphasis away from previously highlighted KPIs between consecutive filings—using embedding-similarity to score semantic drift on metric-focused segments. This approach achieves more than double the risk-adjusted alpha of a named-entity-recognition baseline because it preserves contextual qualifiers (hedging language, forward-looking caveats) that keyword methods discard.

Implication. spawn-candidate: filing-drift variant of news-reactive — score each position candidate by semantic-shift magnitude in its most recent SEC filing; trigger only on significant positive disclosure momentum; 3–5 day hold, long-only

RESEARCH · FITS

LLM Market Timing Collapses Under Broad Cross-Section and Long-Horizon Testing

Li, Kim, Cucuringu & Ma (FINSABER framework, arXiv 2505.07078, revised Feb 2026) evaluated LLM timing strategies across 20 years and 100+ symbols, versus prior evaluations on narrow windows of 5–10 names. LLM-driven timing systematically underperforms passives: overly conservative in bull regimes and overly aggressive in bear regimes, the opposite of the desired profile. The authors attribute narrow-universe wins to survivorship and data-snooping bias and recommend that regime-aware risk controls supersede LLM signal scaling.

Implication. fits existing: news-reactive

RESEARCH · METHODOLOGICAL

Trend-Following on a Strategy's Own Equity Curve Reliably Reduces Sharpe

Allocate Smartly (published May 11, 2026) tested all moving-average parameter combinations (short/long window pairs) as on/off switches applied to 100+ tactical asset allocation strategies. Every combination underperformed the unconditional average strategy on Sharpe ratio; the single outperformer (6/12-month MA) was dismissed as noise at the p-level for the number of combinations tested. Max drawdown improved modestly because switching to cash reduces equity exposure, not because the switch timing added skill.

Implication. out of scope: methodological warning for the evolution pipeline itself — performance-based equity-curve switching degrades risk-adjusted returns; retirement and spawn decisions should use structural criteria (factor exposure, signal decay, regime fit) not trailing equity-curve trend
