RESEARCH · FITS

Short-Term Reversal Contamination Suppresses Industry Momentum

Gao, Li, Yuan & Zhou (SSRN, March 2026) show that standard industry momentum strategies carry hidden short-term reversal exposure that erodes returns. Dynamically hedging this reversal component raises strategy Sharpe from 0.56 to 1.11 on U.S. equity data — nearly doubling risk-adjusted returns without altering the underlying momentum signal. The mechanism: one-month reversals are systematically embedded in cross-industry return co-movement and act as a drag on multi-month momentum continuation.

Implication. fits existing: momentum-trader — add a 1-month reversal hedge/filter to signal construction; when last-month return is in the top decile, downweight the position to neutralise reversal drag before entry

RESEARCH · FITS

Cross-Asset Macro Features Dominate Pure Technicals in Regime-Adaptive ML

A walk-forward study (HarbourFront, April 2026) trained LightGBM on 63 features spanning technical indicators, macro data, and cross-asset signals on the NASDAQ-100 and S&P 500 universes, achieving a Sharpe of 1.18 (95% CI: 0.53–1.84) with an ~17% alpha-positive rate, beating four baselines including XGBoost, logistic regression, and SMA crossover. The counter-intuitive finding: Bitcoin trend and gold/equity ratio ranked as the top regime predictors, outperforming RSI, MACD, and all stock-specific inputs — particularly for high-beta tech names. The model autonomously switched between mean-reversion and risk-appetite logic based on regime classification.

Implication. fits existing: macro-aligned — incorporate BTC 20-day trend and gold/equity ratio as additional regime-signal inputs alongside yield-curve and DXY; candidate LightGBM classifier to replace current rule-based regime router

RESEARCH · FITS

Sentiment Dispersion Outperforms Sentiment Direction in News-Driven Signals

An interpretable-ML study (arXiv:2505.16136, May 2026) applied FinBERT to GDELT news headlines and trained XGBoost on derived features, testing on FX and Treasury futures out-of-sample (Jan 2017–Apr 2025; Sharpe 4.65–5.87 on those instruments). The key SHAP finding — directly transferable to equities — is that sentiment dispersion (spread of opinion across articles) and article impact (sentiment × log volume) are far more predictive than mean sentiment alone. Mean sentiment is cheap and already crowded; cross-article disagreement is not yet widely implemented in retail-accessible pipelines.

Implication. fits existing: news-reactive — replace scalar morning-brief sentiment score with a per-ticker dispersion measure across multiple sources; weight positions by article-impact score (sentiment magnitude × log article count)

RESEARCH · METHODOLOGICAL

LLM-Based Trading Agents Fail to Outperform Buy-and-Hold at 20-Year Scale

A rigorous long-horizon backtest (arXiv:2505.07078, May 2026) evaluated FinMem and FinAgent across 63–91 symbols from 2004–2024 with survivorship and look-ahead controls. Both failed to beat passive benchmarks: FinMem posted Sharpe -0.19 in bull regimes and -0.97 in bear regimes; FinAgent achieved 0.12 and -0.38. Reported alpha was statistically insignificant (p > 0.34 across all tests). The conclusion — 'LLM-derived alpha is likely a methodological artefact of narrow, biased evaluations' — directly challenges the recent trend of LLM-as-trader systems, attributing prior positive results to survivorship bias and sub-cycle backtesting windows.

Implication. out of scope: this is a definitive negative finding — validate that news-reactive uses NLP sentiment features (FinBERT-class embeddings or scored signals) rather than zero-shot LLM trade generation; avoid adding any LLM-call-as-signal layer

RESEARCH · SPAWN CANDIDATE

Elevated Rates Widen Quality-vs-Leverage Dispersion Across Sectors

A May 2026 practitioner report (HedgeCo Insights) links the 2025 outperformance of quant equity funds — D.E. Shaw Oculus +28.2%, Bridgewater Pure Alpha +34%, Point72 +17.5% — to structurally wider cross-stock dispersion driven by the rate environment. The argument: high rates force the market to re-price balance-sheet risk, widening spreads between high-FCF-yield, low-leverage names and their overleveraged sector peers. Dispersion-driven alpha is strongest in Factor Rotation and Market-Neutral Equity books, not broad beta. The effect has been structural since 2022 and is still widening in 2026.

Implication. spawn-candidate: quality-tilted sector-rotator variant — within each sector ETF pick, add interest-coverage ratio and FCF yield as a secondary sort on top of sector-rotator's relative-strength screen; captures the dispersion signal without requiring short legs
