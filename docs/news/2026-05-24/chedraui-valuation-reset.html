Chedraui's fair value cut 14% as analysts reassess retail fundamentals

A Chedraui supermarket in Mexico. The retailer operates hundreds of stores domestically alongside US banners Smart & Final and El Super. — Wikimedia Commons
In one sentence
Chedraui's fair value estimate fell 14% to MX$130.97, driven by P/E multiple compression from 23.56x to 19.30x as Mexican retail fundamentals soften.

Grupo Comercial Chedraui's Class B shares already trade well below most analyst targets. Now those targets have come down to meet them.

The fair value estimate for CHDRAUI B, as compiled from analyst consensus by Simply Wall St, has been cut from MX$152.80 to MX$130.97 — a 14.3% reduction. The ceiling on the price target range dropped simultaneously, from MX$140 to MX$125. With the stock changing hands at roughly MX$98, traders might read this as a narrowing gap. More accurately, it is the models catching up to a share that has already lost significant ground: the 52-week high stands at MX$164.99.

The specific trigger for the reset was not disclosed in the analyst summary. But the model mechanics are visible. The principal driver is P/E multiple compression: the forward earnings multiple embedded in the valuation fell from 23.56x to 19.30x. That is a meaningful de-rating — roughly an 18% reduction in the multiple alone. Partially offsetting that, revenue growth assumptions were nudged upward (from 4.72% to 6.21%) and the net profit margin estimate ticked higher (2.80% to 2.86%). The discount rate rose only marginally, from 15.35% to 15.43%, suggesting no significant change in the perceived risk profile of the business itself.

In other words, the reset looks less like a fundamental alarm and more like an expectation that the market will not pay as much for each peso of earnings as it once did — a sector-level repricing rather than company-specific deterioration.

The macro backdrop that explains the de-rating

The broader context makes that multiple compression legible. Mexico's GDP contracted 0.8% in the first quarter of 2026. ANTAD same-store sales grew just 1.7% year-on-year in March — below the 4%-to-9% range that characterized the post-pandemic years — as consumer confidence weakens amid rising inflation and USMCA uncertainty. Supermarkets and department stores are underperforming specialty formats. For a company of Chedraui's scale, that operating environment limits the argument for a premium multiple.

The US operations add a further complication. Chedraui's American banners — Smart & Final and El Super — were already absorbing FX headwinds when US immigration enforcement began weighing on same-store sales at El Super and Fiesta Mart in the first half of 2026. The company projects margin recovery in the US once its Rancho Cucamonga distribution centre delivers efficiencies, but that tailwind is forecast, not yet in the numbers. Grupo Santander moved to Underperform in December 2025 on similar logic: too little remaining upside at the prevailing price, with execution risks unresolved.

At MX$130.97, the revised fair value estimate still implies more than 30% upside from current levels. The bear case — the MX$125 ceiling of the range — is less generous but still constructive. The question for anyone rebuilding a position is whether the multiple stabilises at 19x or continues to compress if Mexican consumer data stays soft through the summer.

The next read on organic momentum comes with Chedraui's second-quarter results, expected in late July or August. Before that, any revision to Mexico's GDP trajectory or formal escalation in USMCA review talks would shift the argument on the multiple. The dividend — MX$1.0170 per share annually, with tranches in November and December — provides some floor, but at current prices it represents a yield of just over 1%. Thin comfort if the multiple story has further to run.
