The death of the ethical-brand premium

Inflation-squeezed shoppers have abandoned Everlane, Allbirds, and Beyond Meat for cheaper alternatives, collapsing a decade-long investor thesis that virtue commands a lasting price premium.

The premium that markets have paid for ethical and sustainable consumer brands is collapsing, as inflation-squeezed consumers prioritize price over virtue. Allbirds, which raised $2 billion at its September 2021 IPO, has lost roughly 90% of its peak value. Beyond Meat has plummeted from $235 per share in 2019 to under $3. Everlane, the digital-native sustainable fashion brand, underwent multiple workforce cuts and abandoned retail expansion after failing to sustain premium pricing. The pattern accelerated through 2023 and 2024 as consumer discretionary spending tightened. The unraveling signals the end of a decade-long investment thesis that ethical positioning alone could justify premium valuations. Fund managers now face pressure to reassess consumer-staples holdings built on the assumption that sustainability premiums would prove durable.
