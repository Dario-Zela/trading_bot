Small-cap ETFs quietly outperform as investor flows stay thin

Traders on the New York Stock Exchange floor, circa 1963 — Library of Congress / Wikimedia Commons
In one sentence
Small-cap ETFs are outpacing the S&P 500 in 2026 while investors pull cash — a performance-flows divergence that historically precedes larger rotations.

Small-cap exchange-traded funds have posted meaningful outperformance against the S&P 500 in 2026 — and the money has largely not followed. That gap between returns and flows is the data point worth watching.

The Russell 2000 index logged an all-time closing high of 2,718.77 on January 22, led a rotation narrative into February, pulled back into correction territory in March (down 10.9% from that high), and has since stabilised. Year-to-date it has still outpaced the S&P 500 by a wide margin: the index is up roughly 16% against the benchmark's approximately 7.6% gain. At the fund level, IJR — the iShares Core S&P Small-Cap ETF — has returned about 11.6% through mid-May. Vanguard's Small-Cap Growth ETF (VBK) is up roughly 9%.

Flows tell a different story

IWM, the iShares Russell 2000 ETF and the most liquid small-cap vehicle at around $65 billion in assets, has bled roughly $6 billion in net outflows year-to-date. VBK has shed approximately $357 million despite its 9% gain. The Vanguard Small-Cap ETF (VB) pulled in $292 million; State Street's SPSM attracted $455 million. Only IJR stands out with approximately $2.1 billion in net inflows — a respectable number in isolation, but modest for a fund with close to $80 billion under management.

Eric Balchunas of Bloomberg Intelligence has framed the structural context plainly: small-cap ETFs have declined from roughly 10% of U.S. ETF industry assets to closer to 4%, a compression that reflects a decade of capital crowding into mega-cap growth. Small-cap mutual funds have absorbed approximately $25 billion in outflows over a similar period. The investor base is thin, and it has not rebuilt.

The valuation arithmetic

The Russell 2000 currently trades at a trailing price-to-earnings ratio of roughly 18. The S&P 500's equivalent figure is approximately 31. That discount — around 37% on a trailing P/E basis — is near its widest in roughly two decades. Small-cap bears attribute the gap to real risks: higher financial leverage, thinner operating margins, and greater sensitivity to domestic interest rates. Small-cap bulls point to the same number and call it neglect.

The 2026 performance record tilts toward the latter interpretation, at least so far.

The rotation argument, annotated

The thesis that capital would eventually leave crowded mega-cap AI positions for cheaper, domestically oriented small-caps has circulated since late 2024. What's different now is that a performance record — however interrupted by March's correction — has accumulated to support it.

If outflows from IWM reverse materially, the feedback loop becomes self-reinforcing: new capital buys index constituents, index prices rise, performance metrics improve, more capital follows. That sequence has not started. The preconditions are assembling.

Geopolitical volatility complicates the picture. The Iran impasse has pushed Nasdaq down 1% and crude above $106 today, conditions that tend to compress risk appetite rather than rotate it. Institutional rebalancing at the fund level — Third Point's Q1 exits from Microsoft and Nvidia being one example — is a subtler signal: if concentrated mega-cap AI positions are unwinding at the portfolio level, small-cap vehicles are a plausible next destination.

What to watch

Weekly flow data for IWM over the next four to six weeks is the cleanest near-term indicator. A net inflow reversal of even $1–2 billion would be the first clean signal that capital is beginning to follow this year's returns. The Federal Reserve's next rate guidance matters too — smaller domestic companies carry more floating-rate debt, so a steeper yield curve helps their case, while a July rate hike, which several economists now consider possible given the Iran conflict's inflationary impulse, would cut against it.
