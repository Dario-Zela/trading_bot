The Spanish fragrance group's ambitious expansion strategy is left in limbo after Estée Lauder withdrew from what would have been one of the largest deals in the global luxury goods sector, signalling that consolidation headwinds have reached even the high-margin beauty segment.

The failed megadeal signals that consolidation momentum in luxury goods may be stalling, with potential ripple effects on valuations across the sector. For trading desks, it underscores the volatility of M&A-dependent growth narratives and sector exposure in a tighter financing environment.

Key facts:
- Estée Lauder withdrew from merger talks with Puig, ending plans for one of the largest luxury goods sector deals
- Puig's share price declined following the withdrawal announcement
- The Spanish fragrance and beauty group must recalibrate its strategic expansion plans after the deal's collapse
