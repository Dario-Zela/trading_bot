Champions League final: one week to Munich as clubs arrive and tactical previews take over

Puskás Aréna, Budapest: the 67,215-capacity ground hosts the Champions League final for the first time in Hungary's history — Wikimedia Commons
In one sentence
PSG defend their Champions League title against Arsenal at Budapest's Puskás Aréna on May 30 — the first final off free-to-air UK TV since 1992.

The advance guard has arrived in Budapest. Hotels fill, broadcast trucks position themselves around the Puskás Aréna, and sports desks across Europe file the piece they always file in the seven days before a Champions League final: the one about clubs arriving and tactical previews dominating the coverage. Consider this that piece — written, at least, with the self-awareness its genre rarely affords.

Paris Saint-Germain and Arsenal meet on 30 May at 18:00 CEST in the Hungarian capital, the first time the country has hosted European football's premier club final. The Puskás Aréna, which opened in November 2019 on the site of the old Ferenc Puskás Stadion, seats 67,215. PSG arrive as defending champions, attempting something only Real Madrid managed between 2016 and 2018: consecutive titles. Arsenal return to the final for the first time since losing to Barcelona in 2006, their path through the knockouts built on a defence that conceded six goals in 14 Champions League matches — a figure that becomes more striking the longer you look at it.

The one line that earns its place in the preview

The tactical framing between the two managers is, at least, worth the column inch. Luis Enrique offered an admission at his pre-final presser that was either generous or tactical: "Without the ball, they are the best team in Europe." For a PSG side that beat Bayern Munich on aggregate to reach Budapest, that concession carries weight. Enrique also acknowledged that PSG "cannot match Arsenal" in the set-piece department — a vulnerability Arsenal have exploited more than any other side in this season's competition. The counterweight is Ousmane Dembélé, who arrives in Budapest with 33 league goals and eight Champions League strikes; if he finds space in behind Arsenal's defensive line, the margin for error collapses quickly.

The most genuinely newsworthy element of the build-up sits not in the tactical previews but in the broadcast picture. For the first time since 1992-93, the Champions League final will not be available free-to-air in the United Kingdom. TNT Sports, which previously streamed the final via Discovery+ and YouTube, has made this edition exclusive to its pay-TV channel and HBO Max. UEFA's contract reportedly requires "best endeavours" toward free access; no legal obligation exists. With three English clubs in Uefa's men's finals this season — Arsenal in the Champions League, Aston Villa in the Europa League, Crystal Palace in the Conference League — the calculus for a subscriber push was, evidently, irresistible.

Kick-off in Budapest on 30 May. The story arrives after that.
