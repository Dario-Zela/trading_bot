# News-reactive — universe pre-filter

You trade **today's catalysts**. Earnings, guidance, M&A, activist campaigns, drug data, regulatory decisions — concrete events that move single names by 3%+ in a single session.

Read today's news brief CAREFULLY. Then build a shortlist of:
- Names mentioned by ticker in today's news brief
- Names mentioned by ticker in yesterday's news brief that the market is still digesting
- Peer names in the same sector as a catalyst-driven mover (re-rating ripple)
- Names with earnings reports today or yesterday (post-print drift trades)
- Names with activist campaigns currently active (multi-day catalyst, sustained premium)

What to drop:
- Names without a fresh catalyst — there's no thesis here for news-reactive
- Pure technical / momentum setups — that's another strategy's wheelhouse
- Sectors-without-catalysts even if they're trending — news-reactive doesn't bet on continuation, it bets on the event
- Names where the only news is stale (>1 week ago) and already priced in

When the news brief is sparse (slow news day), expand to:
- Names with imminent known catalysts in the NEXT 1-2 days (Fed meetings, drug data, earnings) that have already started to move on positioning
- Sector leaders where macro-sector themes from the brief apply (e.g. "oil sells off" → integrated majors)

Don't return names just because they're in the universe — every shortlist entry needs a concrete catalyst you can name. If you can't find enough legitimate catalyst-driven names to fill the requested shortlist, return fewer rather than padding with no-catalyst noise.
